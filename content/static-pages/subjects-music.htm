There are two teaching rooms which are each fitted with state of the art facilities such as Interactive Whiteboards, Visualisers and keyboards. Room 004 also has a suite of computers with Cubase and midi keyboards installed. These facilities are accessible to pupils both within lessons and after school to assist with independent learning and homework.

The department has a fully equipped recording studio and rehearsal room. In addition, there are four practice rooms including two full drum kits, amps, bass and electric guitars which can be booked for use by pupils before and after school.

There is also a wide stock of African drums, Samba percussion, xylophones, metallophones, acoustic guitars and ukuleles available for pupils to use in lessons and in extra-curricular time.

The Curriculum

Key Stage 3

Pupils in Years 7, 8 & 9 receive one 60 minute lesson a week with homework set fortnightly. Pupils are taught in mixed ability groups with an average class size of 25 or below. Progress is tracked and monitored in accordance with agreed procedure. Pupils complete a baseline assessment on entry in Year 7 and will receive regular composing, performing and listening assessments throughout the year.

Key Stage 4

Pupils in Years 10 & 11 receive three one hour lessons a week with homework set every week with daily instrumental practise expected from every pupil. Extended Curriculum

Revision/Catch-up Sessions

For upper school, the Music department organises revision, rehearsal and recording sessions to support pupils with the preparation of the coursework and studies. All year groups can access the computers and internet facilities which are always available for pupils to use. In addition, pupils can book time before and after school to play on the wide range of instruments available or even a rock band rehearsal.

Enrichment

The department offers lessons on guitar, voice, drums, piano, woodwind, brass and violin and run Music Clubs for GCSE revision, big band, choir and instrumental groups.
