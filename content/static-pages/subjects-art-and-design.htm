The Art rooms are purpose built and well resourced, with a wide range  of art materials and equipment. The classrooms are fitted with state of  the art facilities such as Interactive Whiteboards and Visualisers.   Each room also has a suite of computers and these facilities are  accessible to pupils both within lessons and after school to assist with  independent learning and homework.

The Curriculum

Key Stage 3

Pupils in Years 7, 8 & 9 receive one 60 minute lesson a week with  homework every two weeks, as is the policy at Hollingworth. Pupils are  taught in mixed ability groups with class sizes of 25 or below.   Progress is tracked and monitored in accordance with agreed procedure.   Pupils complete a baseline assessment on entry in Year 7 and will  receive six assessments a year.

Key Stage 4

Pupils in Years 10 & 11 receive three 60 minute lessons a week  with homework/target time once a week.  We offer three different GCSE  two year courses: Unendorsed Art and Design, Graphics, and Textiles (40%  examinations and 60% Controlled Assessment with the AQA examination  board).  Pupils are taught in mixed ability groups.  Progress is tracked  and monitored in accordance with agreed procedure.  Pupils complete a  range of both formal and informal assessments in the form of extended  sketchbook activities in class and also mock and real examinations.

Revision/Catch-up Sessions

For the upper school, the Art department organises support sessions  over several hours in a week, whereby coursework catch up and assessment  guidance is offered.  All year groups can access the computers and  internet facilities in classrooms which are always available for pupils  to use before school, during break and after school.
